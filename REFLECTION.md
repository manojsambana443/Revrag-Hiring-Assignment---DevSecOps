# Reflection

AI helped me quickly identify common issues like hardcoded secrets, use of latest base images, and missing security checks. It also gave me a general direction on how to structure the fixes.

But I didn’t rely on it blindly. In some cases, the suggestions were either too generic or not practical for real scenarios. I had to think through what actually makes sense, especially for the decision questions.

For example, handling vulnerabilities when you can’t fix them immediately is not just about following rules — it’s about balancing risk and stability. That part required my own judgment.

I also made sure I understood every change I applied, because I know I’ll have to explain it later.

Overall, AI was useful as a helper, but I still had to think through the problems and make decisions based on real-world reasoning.
